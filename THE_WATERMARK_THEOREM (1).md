# The Watermark Theorem

### The discriminant of the line lattice of Fermat surfaces of prime degree

**Rafael Amichis Luengo** · Madrid · [tretoef@gmail.com](mailto:tretoef@gmail.com) · [github.com/tretoef-estrella](https://github.com/tretoef-estrella)

## Abstract

Let S_m ⊂ P³ be the complex Fermat surface x₀^m + x₁^m + x₂^m + x₃^m = 0 of prime degree m ≥ 5, and let V be the sublattice of H²(S_m, Z) generated by the classes of its 3m² lines. We prove the closed formula

> **|disc V| = m^{3(m−3)²}.**

Since the lines generate the full Néron–Severi group for gcd(m, 6) = 1 (Schütt–Shioda–van Luijk; Degtyarev), this computes |disc NS(S_m)| exactly for every prime m ≥ 5 and answers, for prime degree, the determinant question raised by Shioda in 1987 (Questions 7.2/7.4 of [7]). The proof is a chain of elementary, fully explicit lattice computations: a pairing lemma by character orthogonality, a trace-lattice discriminant by a Ramanujan-sum circulant, a transport identity, one exactly evaluated determinant, a blockwise saturation count, a kernel-saturation theorem (mK ⊆ SAT), and a 12-dimensional relation count over F_m solved in closed form. Every lemma was verified independently in a second computational environment, and the global identity was confirmed numerically at m = 5, 7 and, through a sealed pre-registered prediction, at m = 11. The companion note [B] derives the corollaries: the discriminant of the transcendental lattice and an explicit closed recipe for the Brauer group order of the reductions of S_m over finite fields.

## 1. Introduction

The Néron–Severi lattice of the Fermat surface has been a touchstone of the theory of algebraic cycles since Shioda's work of 1979–1987. For degree m with gcd(m, 6) = 1, the 3m² lines on S_m generate NS(S_m) integrally — this is the theorem of Schütt–Shioda–van Luijk [4] completed by Degtyarev [5], resolving the question posed by Aoki–Shioda [3]. The *rank* of NS(S_m) has been known in closed form since [3]:

> rank NS(S_m) = 3(m−1)(m−2) + 1.

The *discriminant*, by contrast, resisted closure. Shioda asked for its structure in 1987 ([7], p. 133, Questions 7.2 and 7.4); Schütt–Shioda–van Luijk verified the value |disc NS| = m^{3(m−3)²} computationally for odd m ≤ 81 ([4], around eq. (16)); Aljovin–Movasati–Villaflor [6] computed the elementary divisors of these lattices cell by cell for degrees up to 14 (their Table 1 — the same rows our instruments reproduce byte-exact in §13), at hardware costs reaching 170 GB of swap memory for a single cell, while their largest cells (the linear-cycle lattices behind their Table 2) remained beyond computation. To our knowledge no proof of the closed formula, valid for all prime m, existed in the literature: each published value is an isolated machine verdict.

This note proves it. The argument is deliberately elementary: it never leaves the explicit integer Gram matrix of the line configuration, and every step is either a character-orthogonality computation, a circulant determinant, or a finite-dimensional count over F_m carried out in closed form. The structure of the proof — discriminants of explicit trace sublattices, one big determinant, and the saturation index of the Gram kernel — was discovered by measurement (the campaign's instruments and run logs are part of the public record; see §13) and then closed by pen and paper. The same formula, with the extra factor (m/4)³ for even m, fits all published data and seven sealed blind predictions of the campaign (the *Watermark Law*, [W]); only the odd-prime half is proven here, and the scope is stated precisely in §2.

## 2. The configuration, the lattice, and the statement

**The surface and its lines.** S_m: x₀^m + x₁^m + x₂^m + x₃^m = 0 in P³(C), m ≥ 5 prime. S_m contains exactly 3m² lines [4], organized in three families of m² according to the pairing of coordinates; in the standard parametrization (the one used in [4] and [6]) each family is indexed by (k, l) ∈ (Z/m)². By adjunction every line has self-intersection 2 − m; two distinct lines of the same family meet (in one point) precisely when they share one of their two indices; lines of different families meet precisely when a single linear condition on the indices holds. In the coordinates fixed throughout this note (the campaign's session coordinates, which realize the standard configuration):

- within a family: L_{(k,l)} · L_{(k′,l′)} = 1 if k = k′ or l = l′ (and (k,l) ≠ (k′,l′)), 0 otherwise; self-intersection 2 − m;
- family 1 × family 2: incidence ⟺ k − l ≡ k′ − l′ (mod m);
- family 2 × family 3: incidence ⟺ k + l ≡ k′ + l′ (mod m);
- family 1 × family 3: incidence ⟺ k′ − l′ ≡ k + l + 1 (mod m).

Let A = ⊕_{j=1,2,3} Z^{(Z/m)²} be the free abelian group on the lines and G the 3m² × 3m² intersection Gram above. Define

> **V := A / ker G**,  the *line lattice*, with the induced bilinear form.

All results below are theorems about this explicitly defined integer matrix; the geometric identification of G with the intersection form of the 3m² lines is the classical configuration of [4], [6].

**Theorem (the Watermark Theorem, odd-prime half).** *For every prime m ≥ 5,*

> **|disc V| = m^{3(m−3)²}.**

**Corollary 1 (Shioda's question, prime degree).** *For prime m ≥ 5, gcd(m, 6) = 1, so V = NS(S_m) by [4, Thm 1.1] and [5]; hence |disc NS(S_m)| = m^{3(m−3)²}, answering Questions 7.2/7.4 of [7] for prime degree.*

**Scope.** m = 3 is degenerate for this machinery (the four-pencil argument of Lemma R.2 and the degree count of Lemma C require m ≥ 5) and its disc exponent is 0. Odd composite m and the even half of the Watermark Law (the extra factor (m/4)³) are **not claimed here**: they remain conjectural, supported by the measured evidence catalogued in [W] (all twelve published AMV rows and seven sealed blind predictions).

**Notation.** For t ∈ Z/m put x_t(k, l) = k + t·l, and x_∞(k, l) = l. The m + 1 **pencils** of a family are the level-set partitions of these forms (t ∈ {0, ∞} the two **axis** pencils; the rest **live**). pb_L denotes pullback along x_L; Z₀ ⊂ Z^m the sum-zero sublattice; N_j the all-ones vector of family j; aug(u) = Σ u(p); c_m(x) = m·[x ≡ 0] − 1 the prime-m Ramanujan function; f_r := m·e_r − 1 ∈ Z₀, so c_m(x − r) = f_r(x). The translation torus Γ ≅ (Z/m)³ acts on A by isometries; its characters act on the families through the dictionary fam1 ↔ (a, b, 0), fam2 ↔ (a, b, a+b), fam3 ↔ (a, a, a+b′), and a family pencil corresponds to a punctured line of (Z/m)²-characters. The **trace vectors** are v_{j,(a,b),r} := the vector of family j with coordinates c_m(ak + bl − r).

## 3. Lemma P (pairings, dead axes, phases, census)

Write D_x := Σ_{k∈Z/m} ζ^{xk} = m·[x ≡ 0] and w_j(a,b) := Σ_{k,l} ζ^{ak+bl} L_j(k,l). Splitting each Gram block into its diagonal and incidence parts and summing geometric series gives closed pairing formulas; in particular:

**(P1)** For a, b ≢ 0: w_j(a,b) · w_j(−a,−b) = (2−m)m² − m² − m² = **−m³**, uniformly in m.
**(P2)** For axis characters ((a,0) or (0,b)) the self-pairing vanishes and w ⊥ H (the hyperplane class); negative-definiteness on the primitive part forces the axis classes to vanish in V — the *dead axes*.
**(P-cross)** Blocks (1,2) and (2,3): w_j(a,b) · w_{j′}(a′,b′) = D_{a+a′} D_{a+b} D_{a′+b′}. Block (1,3): the twisted incidence contributes one explicit phase: w₁(a,b) · w₃(a′,b′) = **ζ^{a′}** · D_{a+a′} D_{b+a′} D_{a′+b′}.
**(P3′)** On the three overlap families of Γ-characters (where two family dictionaries collide) the supporting trace classes satisfy the null relations v_{j,r} + v_{j′, r+s₀} ∈ ker G, with phase law s₀ = 0 for the pairs (1,2), (2,3) and s₀ = 1 (the ζ^{a′} phase at function level) for (1,3).
**(P4)** Inclusion–exclusion over the three blocks gives 3(m−1)² − 3(m−1) = **3(m−1)(m−2)** live characters, each of multiplicity one in V ⊗ Q — reproving the rank formula of [3] from scratch.

**Corollary 5 (kernel census).** ker G ⊗ Q = six axis character lines (rank 6(m−1)) ⊕ three overlap null lines (rank 3(m−1)) ⊕ a 2-dimensional trivial part; total rank 9m − 7. In particular a kernel vector has zero isotypic mass on every live non-overlap pencil.

*Verification:* every displayed pairing checked exhaustively over all characters at m = 5 and 7, including the (1,3) phase on all m⁴ cases per prime, in two independent environments.

## 4. Lemma T (the trace-lattice discriminant)

For a live orbit O supported on family j with pencil form (a′, b′), the m − 1 independent trace vectors v_r have Gram B(v_r, v_s) = Σ_t ζ^{−t(r−s)} (w(tα)·w(−tα)) = −m³ · c_m(r − s) by (P1); the (m−1) × (m−1) matrix −m³(mI − J) has |det| = m^{3(m−1)} · m^{m−2}, since det(mI − J)_{m−1} = m^{m−2}. Hence for every live orbit

> **disc T(O) = ±m^{4m−5}.**

## 5. The transport identity

Let K := ker G (saturated, rank 9m − 7) and let F ⊂ A be the Z-span of one trace lattice per live orbit together with the trivial lift N₁. Then F ∩ K = 0 and

> **unified := [V : φ(F)] = [A : F ⊕ K] = |det[ basis of K | generators of F ]|,**

where φ: A → V is the quotient map. Stacking the explicit kernel spawn K₀ (the Lemma-P basis: axis trace systems, paired overlap null systems, N₁ − N₂, N₂ − N₃) instead of a kernel basis measures unified · [K : K₀]; the trivial lift satisfies φ(N₁)² = m³ = m²·e² where e generates the rank-1 trivial piece (e² = m), so φ(N₁) = ±m·e contributes one exact factor m to the bookkeeping.

## 6. The big determinant

> **|det[K₀ | F | N₁]| = m^{(9m²−3m−6)/2}, exactly over Z.**

*Proof.* Under the standard inner product on A: (i) different families have disjoint supports, hence are orthogonal; (ii) vectors built on different character lines within one family are orthogonal, because Σ_{k,l} c_m(ak+bl−r) · c_m(a′k+b′l−s) = (Σ_x c_m)(Σ_y c_m) = 0 for independent forms — the map (k,l) ↦ (x,y) is a bijection of (Z/m)² and Ramanujan sums are sum-zero. The standard Gram of the full stack is therefore block diagonal, and every block is a circulant of the pattern μ(mδ − 1) with det(mI − J)_{m−1} = m^{m−2}: six axis blocks and 3m − 9 live non-overlap blocks contribute m^{3m−4} each (to det²); the three overlap blocks have pattern m²(mI − J) ⊗ [[1,1],[1,2]] with det[[1,1],[1,2]] = 1, contributing m^{6m−8} each; the trivial 3 × 3 block on {N₁−N₂, N₂−N₃, N₁} has ⟨N_i, N_j⟩ = m²δ_{ij}, contributing m⁶. Total exponent 6(3m−4) + (3m−9)(3m−4) + 3(6m−8) + 6 = 9m² − 3m − 6, halved for the determinant. Every block determinant is ± a power of m, so the identity is exact over Z. ∎

*Verification:* v_m = 102 (m = 5) and 207 (m = 7) with non-m part exactly 1, both environments.

## 7. Blockwise saturation

Let SAT ⊂ K be the explicit lattice: the six axis pullback blocks pb_{j,axis}(Z₀); the three paired overlap blocks {pb_j(y) + pb_{j′}(y ∘ τ_{s₀}) : y ∈ Z₀} (τ = cyclic translation, s₀ the P3′ phase); and Z(N₁−N₂) ⊕ Z(N₂−N₃). Membership SAT ⊂ K (which re-proves the phase law at the saturated level) and rank 9m − 7 are file-verified. Each of the nine blocks saturates its trace sub-block by exactly m^{m−2}: the index [Z₀ : span{f_r}] satisfies index² = disc(span f_r)/disc(Z₀) = m^{2m−3}/m, by the same circulant determinant; identically for the paired blocks, whose 2-power disc parts cancel in the ratio. Hence

> **[SAT : K₀] = m^{9(m−2)}.**

## 8. Lemma R (mK ⊆ SAT)

**R.1 (incidence identity).** On each family, Σ_{L over the m+1 pencils} E_L = m·I + J, where (E_L u)(p) = Σ_{p′ ∈ fiber_L(p)} u(p′). *Proof:* a nonzero difference vector of (Z/m)² lies in exactly one of the m + 1 directions; the diagonal is counted m + 1 times. ∎

**R.2 (augmentation divisibility).** For x = (x₁,x₂,x₃) ∈ K: aug(x₁) + aug(x₂) + aug(x₃) = 0 exactly, and m | aug(x_j) for each j. *Proof:* G·N_j = m·1_A by direct incidence counting, giving the exact sum on pairing with x. For the divisibilities, let y be the indicator of the family-1 fiber {k + t·l ≡ c}, t ≠ 0. Within family 1, (Gy)|₁ = 2·1₁ − m·y (for t ≠ 0 the fiber is a graph over k and over l). Across families the incidence condition restricted to the fiber is a bijection except at one exceptional slope per block: G·y = 2·1₁ + 1₂ + 1₃ − m·y for t ∉ {0, 1, −1}; = 2·1₁ + 1₂ + m·z₃ − m·y at t = 1; = 2·1₁ + m·z₂ + 1₃ − m·y at t = −1 (z the displaced fiber indicator in the exceptional family). Such t exists since m ≥ 5. Pairing with x and writing a_j = aug(x_j): 2a₁ + a₂ + a₃ ≡ 0, 2a₁ + a₂ ≡ 0, 2a₁ + a₃ ≡ 0 (mod m); subtracting, a₂ ≡ a₃ ≡ 0 and 2a₁ ≡ 0 with m odd. ∎ (All displayed identities verified entrywise for every t, c, both primes.)

**R.3 (overlap assembly).** For x ∈ K and an overlap pair (L in family j) ↔ (L′ in family j′) with shift s₀: the sum-zero parts of the fiber-sum vectors satisfy f̃_{j′,L′} = f̃_{j,L} ∘ τ_{s₀}. *Proof:* for pencils L″ ≠ L the map p ↦ (x_L(p), x_{L″}(p)) is a bijection, so L-fibers kill L″-isotypic mass; the trivial component contributes only the mean; hence f̃_{j,L} = m·g with pb_L(g) the L-isotypic component of x_j. By the kernel census the only live pencils carrying kernel mass are the overlaps, where the component lies in the one-dimensional null direction of P3′; multiplying the a-th Fourier coefficient by ζ^{a·s₀} is translation by s₀, so g′ = g ∘ τ_{s₀}. The pair pb_j(f̃) + pb_{j′}(f̃ ∘ τ) is then a paired-block member of SAT. ∎

**R.4 (theorem: mK ⊆ SAT).** For x ∈ K, by R.1: m·x_j = Σ_L pb(f_{j,L}) − aug(x_j)·1_j; the fiber means are integers by R.2; writing f = f̃ + mean: m·x_j = Σ_L pb(f̃_{j,L}) + (aug(x_j)/m)·1_j. Axis terms lie in the axis blocks of SAT; live non-overlap sum-zero parts vanish by the census; the six overlap terms group into the three R.3 pairs, members of SAT; and the constant leftover Σ_j (aug_j/m)·1_j has integer coefficients summing to zero (R.2), hence lies in the span of N₁−N₃, N₂−N₃. So **mK ⊆ SAT**. ∎ (Independent fire test: m·x ∈ SAT verified exactly for all 38 kernel basis vectors at m = 5.)

**R.5.** K/SAT is elementary abelian of exponent m; [K : SAT] = m^d with d = (9m−7) − rank_{F_m}(SAT mod m), and **the index has no part at any prime p ≠ m** — by proof, not by measurement.

## 9. Lemma C (the counting lemma: d = 12)

> **rank_{F_m}(SAT mod m) = 9m − 19; equivalently d = dim_{F_m} K/SAT = 12, for every prime m ≥ 5.**

*Proof.* Functions F_m → F_m are uniquely polynomials of degree ≤ m − 1, and Σ_{x∈F_m} x^e = −1 if (m−1) | e, e > 0, and 0 otherwise; hence the mod-m span of the sum-zero column functions is exactly the polynomials of degree ≤ m − 2. An F_m-relation among the SAT columns is a choice of polynomials u_{j,k}, u_{j,l} (axis blocks), w₁₂, w₂₃, w₁₃ (overlap blocks; one polynomial feeds both supporting families, the (1,3) block through the translation τ), and scalars ε₁, ε₂ (the N-differences), satisfying three identities in F_m[k,l] of degree ≤ m − 2 < m (so the function–polynomial dictionary is faithful):

(F1) u_{1,k}(k) + u_{1,l}(l) + w₁₂(k−l) + w₁₃(k+l) + ε₁ = 0
(F2) u_{2,k}(k) + u_{2,l}(l) + w₁₂(k−l) + w₂₃(k+l) + (ε₂ − ε₁) = 0
(F3) u_{3,k}(k) + u_{3,l}(l) + w₂₃(k+l) + (w₁₃ ∘ τ)(k−l) − ε₂ = 0.

Mixed monomials k^{d−r} l^r (1 ≤ r ≤ d−1) arise only from the two live-pencil terms; in degree d their coefficient is C(d,r)·[b_d + (−1)^r a_d], and C(d,r) ≢ 0 (mod m) for d ≤ m − 2. For d ≥ 3 the cases r = 1, 2 force a_d = b_d = 0; for d = 2 the single case r = 1 forces equality of the two quadratic coefficients, and chaining (F1)–(F3) (translation preserves the leading coefficient) makes all three equal to one common q — whose realizability is the polarization identity (k−l)² + (k+l)² = 2k² + 2l². Once the mixed terms cancel, the remaining content of each identity is k-pure plus l-pure, determining each family's axis pair up to its constant ambiguity (c, −c); the determined parts have degree ≤ 2 ≤ m − 2. The solution space is therefore parametrized freely by: the common quadratic q (1); the linear coefficients of the three w's (3); their constants (3); ε₁, ε₂ (2); the three constant splits (3) — total **12**, for every prime m ≥ 5. ∎

*Verification:* direct rank measurements 26 and 44 (= 9m − 19) by two independent constructions, plus the twelve relations exhibited explicitly (file COUNTING_LEMMA_PROBE_v1.py) and checked valid of rank 12 at both primes.

**Hence [K : K₀] = [K : SAT]·[SAT : K₀] = m^{12} · m^{9(m−2)} = m^{9m−6}.**

## 10. Assembly: proof of the Theorem

By §§5–9, unified = |det| / (m · [K : K₀]) = m^{(9m²−3m−6)/2 − 1 − (9m−6)} = m^{(9m²−21m+4)/2}. By §4 and Lemma T,

disc V = disc(⊕_O T(O) ⊕ Z·e) / unified² = (m^{4m−5})^{3(m−2)} · m / m^{9m²−21m+4} = m^{(12m²−39m+30) + 1 − (9m²−21m+4)} = **m^{3m²−18m+27} = m^{3(m−3)²}.** ∎

(The orbit count is 3(m−2) = 3(m−1)(m−2)/φ(m) for prime m.)

## 11. Sign and small cases

V contains the ample hyperplane class, so its form has hyperbolic signature (1, rank V − 1) and disc V = (−1)^{rank V − 1} |disc V| with rank V = 3(m−1)(m−2) + 1. m = 3: the exponent 3(m−3)² is 0 and disc V = ±1 (classical; outside this proof's machinery). Odd composite and even degrees: conjectural — see [W].

## 12. Corollaries beyond the statement

(i) **Shioda's Questions 7.2/7.4** ([7], p. 133) are answered for prime degree (Corollary 1 above). (ii) **The transcendental lattice** T_m = NS^⊥ ⊂ H²(S_m, Z) has |disc T_m| = m^{3(m−3)²} and rank (m−1)(m−3)² — see the companion note [B], where this is derived from unimodularity of H² in five lines. (iii) **The Brauer group** of the reductions of S_m over finite fields acquires a closed-form order recipe through the Artin–Tate formula, every ingredient of which is now explicit — see [B]. (iv) The glue group V/(⊕ pieces) is proven elementary abelian of F_m-dimension (3m²−9m+4)/2, a structure result on the integral cohomology of independent interest.
