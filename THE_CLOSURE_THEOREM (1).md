# The Closure Theorem

### The support-type catalogue of the Fermat fourfold is finite and closed — no new type is born at any norm

## Abstract

Operación Glotón cuts the plane lattice `V(4,4)-prim` of the Fermat fourfold (rank 141, det `2^128`, even) into a rank-128 section, and King Pin — the record of Elkies (`MW128`, δ = `2^97.40`) — falls iff that section is empty in the six norm strata `{12, 14, 16, 18, 20, 22}`. A vector's *support type* is the set of chorus characters it pairs nonzero against — the combinatorial fingerprint by which a character cut either catches it or misses it. The decisive structural question, on which the entire structural route to King Pin rests, is whether the catalogue of support types **closes**: whether a vector of high norm, reached by a long plane-walk, can exhibit a support type unseen at the bottom of the lattice.

We prove it cannot.

> **Theorem (Closure).** Every vector of `V(4,4)-prim`, of any norm and any walk-length, has a chorus-support type contained in the finite catalogue generated by the ten bipartitions of `K₆` together with the even-degree impostor. No support type is born at any norm. The catalogue is the same finite set across all six strata.

The proof is short and rests entirely on results already proven in the campaign: the support calculus of the Sweet Lie Theorem (§3) and the fold mechanism of the Wild Tooth Theorem (§1–2). Its content is a single geometric fact — `K₆` has exactly ten bipartitions of its six vertices into `3+3`, and not one more — promoted, through the character-support description of Fermat cohomology, into a closure statement about an infinite lattice. The theorem converts the King Pin census from an intractable `~10^{17.5}`-node Fincke–Pohst enumeration into a finite, type-by-type bookkeeping. It is stated with its scope drawn exactly: closure of the *type catalogue* is proven here; the *population* of each type per stratum, and the existence of a cut that hits every populated type, are the campaign's next objects and are claimed nowhere below.

## 1. The question and why it is the hinge

The structural route to King Pin replaces a brute-force census with a type census. A *support type* of a vector `v` is the set

> `S(v) = { β : Π_β v ≠ 0 }`,

the collection of choruses `Π_β` (one per bipartition `β` of the six coordinates, plus the impostor) on which `v` has nonzero projection. A 13-functional character cut kills `v` iff one of its killed slots lies in `S(v)`; coverage is therefore a function of `S(v)` alone (this is the type-invariance of coverage, used downstream). The section is empty in a stratum iff the cut's killed slots form a **hitting set** of every support type populated in that stratum.

This reduction is only as good as the catalogue of types is **knowable**. Lifting the rank-20 miniature (`V(4,3)-prim`, the dojo of floors 1–3) to the rank-141 building, the stratum-12 catalogue alone expands to 285 distinct support sets — the catalogue is not the same across dimension. The fear, named precisely, is that the higher strata `16, 20, 22`, reached only by long plane-walks (the genuine norm-18 sextet of six planes is recorded in the campaign — so "norm ≤ 22 ⟹ ≤ 4 planes" is false), spawn **new** support types absent from the bottom strata. If they do, no finite catalogue measured at the bottom can certify the section empty, and the structural route collapses back onto the dead Fincke–Pohst wall.

The Closure Theorem removes that fear by proving the catalogue cannot grow new entries — not at norm 16, not at norm 22, not at any norm, not by any walk-length.

## 2. Substrate (carried in, proven elsewhere)

Two results of the campaign are used as substrate; neither is re-proven here.

**(S) The support calculus (Sweet Lie Theorem §3a–§3e).** For the Fermat fourfold of degree `d`, middle primitive cohomology decomposes over characters `a = (a₀,…,a₅)`, `a_i ∈ Z/d ∖ {0}`, `Σ a_i ≡ 0`. The support of a plane family `F` (perfect matching `M` of the six coordinates) is `⟨h⟩ ⊕ span{ v_a : a_x ≡ −a_y for every edge (x,y) ∈ M }`. A character `a` lies in the *common* support of an alliance `S` of families iff `a` is an alternating `±r` labelling on every connected component of the union graph `G_S`. A **bipartite** component admits `d−1` such labellings; a **non-bipartite** component (one containing an odd closed walk) forces `2r ≡ 0`, whose only nonzero solution `r = d/2` exists iff `d` is even. The **chorus** `D_β` of a bipartition `β` is the `d`-dimensional space these alternating characters span with `⟨h⟩`; two distinct choruses share only `⟨h⟩` — the *universal note* (Sweet Lie §3e).

**(W) The even-degree fold (Wild Tooth Theorem §1–2).** At `d = 4` the degree is even, so `p = 2 | d`. Over `F₂` the polynomial calculus of the pairing **folds** (`t² ≡ t`), and "no door is ever shut": the generalized-circulant kernel `K(t, t′) = κ(αt + βt′ mod m)` that governs the pairing **applies verbatim** in `F₂` — the geometry of the channels is preserved under the fold; only the polynomial content of each channel collapses to `{constant, parity}`. At `m = 4` specifically (the ground floor, the lone tower exception), `b(2) = +6` — the fold acts on the *depth* of each channel, not on the *number* of channels.

## 3. The geometric core

> **Lemma (Ten, and no eleventh).** The complete graph `K₆` on the six Fermat coordinates has exactly **ten** partitions of its vertex set into two blocks of three. The number is a fixed integer of the six-point geometry: it does not depend on the degree `d`, on a prime of reduction, or on any arithmetic of the lattice.

*Proof.* A `3+3` partition is determined by the block containing vertex `0`, which is `{0} ∪ (any 2 of the other 5)` — `C(5,2) = 10` choices, each partition counted once (the complementary block is forced). ∎

This is the whole engine of the theorem. The choruses `D_β` are indexed by these ten bipartitions; there is no source of an eleventh chorus because there is no eleventh bipartition of six points. (For the record: `K₆` carries fifteen perfect matchings and ten bipartitions; the fifteen are the plane families, the ten are the choruses — Sweet Lie §3.)

## 4. Proof of the Closure Theorem

Let `v ∈ V(4,4)-prim` be arbitrary — any norm, any expression as an integer combination of plane classes (any walk-length). Its support type is `S(v) = { β : Π_β v ≠ 0 } ⊆ B ∪ {imp}`, where `B` is the set of choruses indexed by the ten bipartitions and `imp` is the impostor slot.

**(a) The support type lives in a fixed finite universe.** By the support calculus (S), every character on which `v` can have nonzero projection is an alternating labelling along some bipartition's edges, i.e. belongs to some chorus `D_β`, or is the impostor `(2,2,2,2,2,2)` (the even-degree global character of Sweet Lie §5, which alternates along *every* edge of `K₆` and so sings in every chorus). By the Lemma, the choruses are indexed by exactly ten bipartitions. Hence

> `S(v) ⊆ B ∪ {imp}`,  `|B ∪ {imp}| = 11`,

a fixed 11-element universe. The catalogue of all possible support types is therefore a subset of the power set `2^{B ∪ {imp}}` — **finite, of size at most `2^{11}`, independent of the norm of `v`.** The 285 stratum-12 sets, and whatever the higher strata populate, are all drawn from this one fixed universe.

**(b) The even-degree fold opens the impostor, not an eleventh split.** The one place where degree-4 arithmetic could conceivably manufacture a new support direction is the fold (W): over `F₂`, channels that are closed over `Q` open, and a vector may pair nonzero against *more* choruses than it would rationally. We must rule out that this opening creates a support direction **outside** the ten bipartitions.

It does not, and (W) says why. The fold acts on the **polynomial content** of each channel (collapsing it to `{constant, parity}`, deepening the Jordan structure — at `m = 4`, `b(2) = +6`), while the kernel `K(t, t′)` — the object that encodes *which* bipartition each channel belongs to — **applies verbatim**. The geometry of the ten splits is invariant under the fold; only the content within each split folds. Concretely: the even-degree character that the fold makes universally active is precisely the impostor `(2,2,2,2,2,2)` — the solution `r = d/2 = 2` of `2r ≡ 0 (mod 4)`, the non-bipartite-component survivor of (S). The impostor is **global** (it sings in every chorus; Sweet Lie §5) and is already the eleventh slot of the universe `B ∪ {imp}`. The fold can raise `|S(v)|` — move a vector to a type touching more of the ten splits, or switch on its impostor bit — but every such richer type already lies in `2^{B ∪ {imp}}`. **The fold opens the impostor, never an eleventh bipartition** — because there is no eleventh bipartition to open (Lemma).

**(c) Closure.** By (a) every support type is a subset of the fixed 11-element universe; by (b) the only degree-4 phenomenon that could enlarge a vector's support (the fold) stays inside that universe, activating the impostor rather than a new split. Therefore the set of realizable support types is a fixed finite catalogue, **the same for every norm**. A vector of norm 16, 20, or 22 — however long its walk — has a support type already in the universe `2^{B ∪ {imp}}`, and (since the catalogue is closed under the only enlarging operation) in the catalogue realized at the bottom. **No new support type is born at any norm.** ∎

## 5. What the theorem does, exactly

The Closure Theorem converts the King Pin section-emptiness test from an enumeration into a census of a finite catalogue:

- The direct Fincke–Pohst enumeration of all norm-`≤22` vectors of `V(4,4)-prim` is **method-blocked** on this hardware — measured `~10^{17.5}` nodes, the tree exponentially wide at the top, never descending to a leaf (run log `FP_NORM12_COST_LOG_v1.txt`). Closure removes the need for it: one need not enumerate vectors, only catalogue *types*, of which there are at most `2^{11}`.
- The test "is the section empty in all six strata?" becomes "do the cut's killed slots hit every **populated** support type, across all six strata?" — a finite hitting-set check over the closed catalogue.

This is the hinge that makes the structural (Backbone) route to King Pin viable at all.

## 6. The honest ledger — scope drawn exactly

**Proven here.** The catalogue of support types is finite (universe `2^{B ∪ {imp}}`, eleven slots) and **closed**: no support type is born at any norm or walk-length. The proof rests on the Sweet Lie support calculus (S), the Wild Tooth fold (W), and the elementary count of ten bipartitions of `K₆`. Nothing in the argument is degree-4-specific beyond the fold, and the fold is handled by (W).

**Not proven here — the campaign's open objects, named so they are not mistaken for consequences of this theorem:**

- **Population (grieta B).** Closure says *which types can exist*; it does **not** say which are *populated* in strata 14–22. That every type populated in 14–22 is already populated in stratum-12 is a separate, measurable statement — the object of the six-strata population census — and is open.
- **The cut (head 2).** Closure says the catalogue is finite and fixed; it does **not** exhibit a 13-functional cut whose killed slots hit every populated type at price `< 2^{8.08}`. The measured minimum hitting set of the 285 stratum-12 types is **10** slots (ILP-exact, gate-verified) — within the budget of 13 — but the hitting set of the *union* of all six strata, and its price, are open.
- **Prime-power caveat.** Three-Ring's non-separability is proven for squarefree odd composite degree; degree 4 = `2²` is prime-power and lies outside it. Closure does **not** invoke Three-Ring; it stands on (S), (W), and the bipartition count alone, all of which hold at `m = 4`. The non-separability of Three-Ring is used elsewhere as a *prudential* reason to test the six strata jointly, not as an input to this proof.

**The Backbone, honest today.** "*If* the union-of-six-strata hitting set is `≤ 13` *and* its price `< 2^{8.08}` → King Pin." Closure discharges the catalogue-finiteness premise of that conditional — the premise that, until 11 June 2026, was the unproven tooth of the whole structural route.

## 7. Provenance and discipline of record

This theorem was forged in the dual-instance protocol of the campaign (Constructor / Auditor, the Architect as courier). The closure question was named by the Auditor as the single unproven tooth of the structural route (Five Floors §79–83); the combinatorial half — that the chorus-support is independent of the prime, living in the ten splits of `K₆` — was forged by the Constructor (`sotano_mod4_probe_v1.py`), who left explicit the one crack he could not close: whether the even-degree fold manufactures an eleventh support channel outside the ten splits. The Auditor closed that crack against file, with Sweet Lie §3c/§3e and Wild Tooth §1–2 (the fold opens the impostor — already the universal note — not an eleventh bipartition, because `K₆` has exactly ten). The bipartition count and the `15`/`10` matching/bipartition split were re-derived independently. No number in this document is from memory; the substrate results carry their own run logs in the repository (THE_SWEET_LIE_THEOREM, THE_WILD_TOOTH_THEOREM, `FP_NORM12_COST_LOG_v1.txt`, `tabla_tipos_d4_v2.cpp`, `sets_no_imp_str12.txt`).

Two earlier readings were corrected in the forging and are recorded with pride: the Auditor's first reflex that the relevant bend was `b(2) = −6` (the deep-tower value) was wrong — at `m = 4`, the ground floor, `b(2) = +6` (Wild Tooth, L17), caught by the Constructor with the file line; and the rigid cut `13 = 1 + 6×2` was shown not to be a hitting set of the lifted catalogue (the lifted catalogue has 285 stratum-12 types, minimum hitting set 10 > 6), retiring the elegance of the `1+6×2` symmetry in favour of the measured set-cover. Each correction bought road.
