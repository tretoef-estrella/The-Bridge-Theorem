## Abstract

For the Fermat surface `S_m ⊂ P³` of prime degree `m ≥ 5`, we determine the discriminant group of the Néron–Severi lattice `V = NS(S_m)` completely:

> **Theorem (Double Ladder).** `V*/V ≅ (Z/m)^{3m²−24m+59} × (Z/m²)^{3m−16}` for every prime `m ≥ 7`, and `V*/V ≅ (Z/5)^{10} × (Z/25)` for `m = 5`.

The Watermark Theorem (companion document) gives the *order* of this group, `m^{3(m−3)²}`; the present theorem gives its *structure* — together they answer, for prime degree, Question 7.4 of Shioda (1987) in full: not only the determinant of `NS(S_m)`, but its discriminant form's underlying group, hence (with the order) the complete elementary-divisor profile of the lattice. Since `H²(S_m, Z)` is unimodular, the transcendental lattice `T_m` carries the same discriminant group. The proof is a chain of explicit lattice computations on the integer Gram matrix of the `3m²` lines, organised by two new structural objects: **nine affine "shadow" classes** whose evaluation matrix has determinant **6** — a unit for every prime `m ≥ 5` precisely because `gcd(m, 6) = 1`, the same hypothesis under which the lines generate `NS` — and a **rank ladder** `rank(Q|𝔪^K) = 12(m−3) − 3K(K+1)` over a moment filtration, whose rung `K = 2` closes the count. Every law was measured at three primes before being proven, the `m = 11` profile was decided by a sealed prediction written in advance, and every lemma was reproduced independently in a second environment.

## 1. The question and the answer

Shioda, closing his 1987 paper on Jacobi sums [7], asked (Questions 7.2/7.4) for the determinant — and, behind it, the discriminant form — of the Néron–Severi lattice of the Fermat surface. For `gcd(m, 6) = 1` the lattice is generated by the `3m²` lines on the surface (Schütt–Shioda–van Luijk [4], completed by Degtyarev [5]), so the question is in principle a finite computation for each `m` — and Aljovin–Movasati–Villaflor [6] carried that computation out cell by cell within their hardware's reach, tabulating the elementary divisors for the surfaces of degree up to 14 (their Table 1 supplies two of the three anchors below) at costs reaching 170 GB of swap memory for a single cell, while stating that their largest cells (the linear-cycle lattices behind their Table 2) were beyond computation. What no source provides — neither there nor anywhere we could locate — is a *closed law* valid for every prime, or a *proof*: each published value is an isolated machine verdict. The present theorem supplies both, and extends the answer past every table (the `m = 11` profile below was sealed blind; nothing beyond `m = 14` exists in print).

The Watermark Theorem proved the order: `|V*/V| = m^{3(m−3)²}` for every prime `m ≥ 5`. Since the exponent of the group divides `m²` (§3 below), the group is `(Z/m)^a × (Z/m²)^b` with `a + 2b = 3(m−3)²`, and the entire remaining question is **one integer**:

> `b = #(cyclic factors of order m²)`.

**The Double Ladder Theorem asserts `b = 3m − 16` for `m ≥ 7` and `b = 1` for `m = 5`** — whence `a = 3m² − 24m + 59` (resp. `10`).

Three independent anchors. (1) At `m = 5` the profile `(Z/5)^{10} × Z/25` reproduces the published AMV row. (2) At `m = 7`: `(Z/7)^{38} × (Z/49)^5`, matching the AMV row. (3) At `m = 11` — outside every published table — the full profile `(Z/11)^{158} × (Z/121)^{17}` was written down as a sealed prediction *before* the computation and met exactly on all counts.

## 2. Notation and substrate (results carried in from the Watermark chain)

`A = Z^{3m²}` is the free module on the lines, `G` its integer Gram matrix, `φ: A ↠ V` the class map with kernel `K` (saturated). The character orbits split the configuration into `3(m−2)` **pieces**: for each orbit `O` with pencil `L`, `T(O) ⊆ S(O) := V ∩ (piece)` are the trace lattice and its saturation. From the Watermark campaign and the first phase of this operation, the following are **theorems** (proofs in the companion document and in the assembly; instruments `GOLLUM_PROBE_1–3`):

- **Flat law.** `disc S(O) = m^{2m−5}` for every piece, every prime `m ≥ 5`; the dual group `S(O)*/S(O)` is the cyclic module `O/λ^{2m−5}` of the cyclotomic ring (`λ = (1−ζ)`), with abelian profile `(Z/m)³ × (Z/m²)^{m−4}` — by the λ-arithmetic `2m−5 = (m−1) + (m−4)`. (Cyclicity in one line: `S(O)*/S(O)` is a subquotient of `T(O)*/T(O)`, which is cyclic over `O` by the circulant presentation of the trace lattice — Lemma T of the Watermark chain — and a subquotient of a cyclic module is cyclic.)
- **Glue law.** `H := V/L` for `L := ⊕_O S(O) ⊕ Ze` is elementary `m`-torsion of dimension `g = (3m²−9m+4)/2` over `F_m`.
- **The cap.** `[S(O) : T(O)] = m^m` exactly — the saturation steps are the **jewel identity** `t_r + N_j = m·pb_L(e_r)` (so the saturated generators are fiber-indicator classes, computable in `A` with no kernel extraction) and the linear weight `Σ_r r·v_r ∈ m²V`, with the cap proven by a five-pencil polynomial analysis in the register of the Watermark's Lemma C.
- **Lemma D (dictionary).** `K` saturated ⇒ `A ≅ K ⊕ V` ⇒ `coker(G) ≅ (V*/V) ⊕ Z^{9m−7}`: the Smith normal form of the explicit Gram matrix computes the discriminant group of `NS` exactly. (This is what makes the sealed `m = 11` measurement *theorem-backed*.)

Write `D := L*/L` (exponent `m²`), `t := #(m²-cyclics of D) = 3(m−2)(m−4)`, `H_top := H ∩ mD`, `α := dim_{F_m} H_top`, and let `B₂` be the perfect `F_m`-bilinear form on `mD ≅ F_m^t` induced by the `m²`-part of the discriminant pairing.

## 3. Lemma E — the height formula

> **Lemma E.** `b = t − 2α + ρ₂`, where `ρ₂ := rank(B₂|_{H_top})`.

*Proof sketch (full text in the assembly).* By overlattice theory `V*/V ≅ H^⊥/H` inside `D`, and `b = dim_{F_m} m·(H^⊥/H) = dim mH^⊥ − dim(mH^⊥ ∩ H)`. The key computation is the radical of the `(1/m)`-pairing on `D[m]`: it equals `mD`, because the induced form on `D[m]/mD` — per piece, the three top λ-grades of the cyclic module `O/λ^{2m−5}`, plus the line `F_m·e` — is the standard duality of a cyclic module: grade `i` pairs with grade `2−i`, an anti-triangular matrix with unit antidiagonal, and `e` contributes the unit `(1/m)·m`. Hence `z ∈ mH^⊥ ⟺ B₂(z, H_top) = 0`, giving `dim mH^⊥ = t − α` by perfectness of `B₂`; and `mH^⊥ ∩ H` is exactly the `B₂`-radical of `H_top`, of dimension `α − ρ₂`. Subtract. ∎

**Gate of record.** An end-to-end instrument computing `H^⊥/H` directly — knowing nothing of the formula — returns `b = 1` (`m = 5`) and `b = 5` (`m = 7`), the values the formula yields (`GOLLUM_PROBE_7`).

The theorem is now two invariants: `α` and `ρ₂`.

## 4. First invariant — the nine affine shadows and the autograph determinant 6

> **The affine shadow law.** The class of the line at position `(k, l)` of family `j`, taken modulo `mD`, is `u_j + k·v_j + l·w_j` — an *affine* function of position. The nine classes `{u_j, v_j, w_j}` are linearly independent for every prime `m ≥ 5`; hence the glue's image in `D/mD` is exactly their 9-dimensional span, and
> **`α = g − 9 = (3m²−9m−14)/2` for all `m ≥ 5`.**

Affineness follows from the fiber identities of the Watermark's Lemma R: a line's pairing vector against a piece basis is (cross-family constants) + (`m·δ` terms); the deep `m·δ` parts vanish modulo `mD`, and what remains — constants and values of the linear function `y_lin` — is affine in `(k, l)`. Affine maps in two variables over three families cap the span at 9. Independence is sealed by an explicit dual evaluation: because the piece Gram is `≡ 0 (mod m)` (entries `2m − m²δ`), the classes `pb_L(e_r)/m` and `a₀/m` are canonical order-`m` functionals; three "e-readers" (which annihilate all six increments) and six "σ-readers" (which read the pencil slope `(a, b)` on the increments) assemble a block-triangular `9×9` evaluation matrix with

> **determinant = 6, the same integer at every prime** (measured `1 ≡ 6 mod 5`, then `6, 6, 6` at `m = 5, 7, 11, 13`) —

a unit for every prime `m ≥ 5` precisely because `gcd(6, m) = 1`: the seal closes on the same hypothesis (`gcd(m, 6) = 1`) under which the lines generate `NS`. Verified entrywise for every line at two primes; the determinant at four.

## 5. The form is the configuration — `B₂` is the line Gram

By §4, `H_top` is described explicitly: combinations whose coefficient function on each family has **zero mass, zero k-moment, zero l-moment** modulo `m`. For such a combination `c`, the fiber identities force `⟨v_c, L⟩ ⊆ mZ`, so `v_c/m ∈ L*` is a legitimate representative of `(1/m)`-classes — and the abstract discriminant form collapses:

> **`B₂(z, z′) = cᵀ G c′ (mod m)` — the line-configuration Gram matrix itself.**

No dual frames, no normalizations: the form the theorem needs is the matrix the configuration *is*. Measured rank: `8 / 30 / 78` at `m = 5 / 7 / 11`, each value declared before the run (`GOLLUM_PROBE_8/10`).

## 6. Second invariant — the rank ladder

Modulo `m`, `G` is pure affine-line incidence: the diagonal blocks are exactly `1(k=k′) + 1(l=l′)` — in one line from the Watermark's Lemma P: same-family pairings are `−(m−2) ≡ 2` (self), `1` (one position shared), `0` (disjoint), which is `1(k=k′) + 1(l=l′)` mod `m` — and the cross blocks are the single affine lines `{k−l}`, `{k+l}`, and one twisted line. Consequently the quadratic form `Q(c) = cᵀGc mod m`:

1. **factors exactly through twelve one-variable "shadows"** — the pushforwards of each family's coefficient function along the four pencil directions `{k, l, k−l, k+l}` (`Q ≡ 0` on the shadow kernel, verified at every filtration level, every prime). These are the **twelve** of the Watermark's Lemma C, returning as the carrier space of the form;
2. has shadow image of codimension 6 per family, cut by the **trinity relations**: three mass equalities, two linear compatibilities, and **the polarization identity `(k−l)² + (k+l)² = 2k² + 2l²`** — Lemma C's lone quadratic survivor, now closing the dimension count: `dim(image at moment-level K) = 12m − 18 − 3K(K+1)/2`, exact at three primes;
3. is **perfect on the assembled shadow space** (axis self-pairings are standard one-variable inner products; the cross channels are hyperbolic pairs), with `W^⊥ ⊆ W` — deficit equal to the relation count, exact at three primes.

These three facts give, for the moment filtration `𝔪^K` (zero moments up to total degree `< K`):

> **The ladder.** `rank(Q|𝔪^K) = 2·dim(image) − 12m = 12(m−3) − 3K(K+1)` for `m ≥ 7` (clamped at 0). Measured rungs: `48/42/30/12/0` (`m = 7`) and `96/90/78/60/36` (`m = 11`), every rung exact.

`H_top` is precisely the level `K = 2`, whence **`ρ₂ = 12m − 54` for `m ≥ 7`**. The drop per rung is `6K = 2 × 3K`; the factor `2` is the rank of a Toeplitz block `u_i·u_j ≡ (m−7)/2 − (i+j)` discovered en route (instrument `GOLLUM_PROBE_9`) — the two halves of the proof are one mechanism, and the theorem takes its name from this: a double mechanism, read on a ladder.

**The `m = 5` boundary, stated once.** At `m = 5` a relation collision at the boundary frees `+2` uniformly across the entire ladder (`26/20/8/0/0` against the clamped law), giving `ρ₂ = α = 8`: the form `B₂` is *nondegenerate* on `H_top`. The same rank-saturation frontier appears in all three places the small prime deviates (the profile, the ladder, the radical) — it is one phenomenon, not three exceptions.
