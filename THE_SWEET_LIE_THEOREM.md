# The Sweet Lie Theorem
### The Möbius quantization of the Fermat fourfold rank — the families lip-sync; the song belongs to the stage
Rafael Amichis Luengo · Madrid · tretoef@gmail.com · github.com/tretoef-estrella · 6 June 2026 · Companion to THE_WATERMARK_THEOREM and THE_DOUBLE_LADDER_THEOREM (same repository). Computations: MacBook Air M2, 8 GB RAM, single thread, 25% CPU.

## Abstract

For the Fermat fourfold X_d ⊂ P⁵ of degree d ≥ 3, let V be the lattice spanned by the classes of its 15·d³ standard planes — fifteen families, one per perfect matching of the six coordinates, with d³ translates each. For a subset S of the families define the Möbius interaction mass ν(S) = Σ_{T⊆S} (−1)^{|S\T|} rank V(T). We prove:

**Theorem (Sweet Lie).** Let G_S be the union graph on the six coordinates of the matchings in S, with components classified bipartite / non-bipartite, and let N_d(G_S) be the product over components of (d−1) for a bipartite component and ε_d for a non-bipartite one, where ε_d = 1 if d is even and 0 if d is odd. Then for every nonempty S and every degree d ≥ 3, prime or composite:

> **ν(S) = (−1)^{|S|+1} · ( 1 + N_d(G_S) ).**

Telescoping over all 2¹⁵ subsets yields, in both parity branches as polynomial identities,

> **rank V(4,d) = 15d³ − 90d² + 175d − 99 + δ·(15d − 39), δ = (d−1) mod 2,**

which equals DS₄(d) + 1, the rank value of Degtyarev–Shimada's Remark 4.4 plus the hyperplane class. The theorem hands every constant of that formula to a finger count over the geometry of K₆ — fifteen matchings, forty-five pencil and sixty skew pairs, an alternating binomial sea, and ten bipartite hexads (the matching-sets of the ten coordinate bipartitions, each a K₃,₃) — and identifies the mechanism: **resonance is bipartiteness.** The name records the discovery that drove the proof: the families appear to sing, but every resonant dimension they exhibit belongs to a coordinate bipartition — the stage — and in even degree one global impostor character sings in every choir at once. Every law below was measured before it was proven, five complete kingdoms (all 156 subset-types at d = 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 — 775 nonempty type-measurements) anchor the statement with not one off-formula value, the even-degree corollary was declared from the proof and then confirmed blind, and the graveyard of dead candidate laws is published with its falsifiers as part of the record.

## 1. The question and the answer

Degtyarev–Shimada [1] proved, via their criterion, a closed rank formula for the lattice generated by the planes of the Fermat fourfold (their Remark 4.4); Aljovin–Movasati–Villaflor [4] computed the lattices cell by cell within their hardware's reach (their fourfold rows stop at d = 6, at costs reaching 170 GB of swap for a single cell). What no source provides is the **interaction structure** behind the formula: how the rank of an arbitrary alliance of plane families is assembled, which alliances carry more than their parts, and why the formula's coefficients are the integers they are. The present theorem answers this by computing the full Möbius spectrum of the rank function on the lattice of family subsets — a 2¹⁵-point object compressed, by S₆-symmetry, to 156 types — and finding it **quantized**: for odd degree, every alliance of three or more families interacts through either exactly one shared dimension or exactly d, nothing in between; the alliances that carry d are precisely those whose combinatorial footprint is bipartite. The rank formula of [1] then falls out by telescoping, with every coefficient a count over K₆.

Three layers of evidence anchor the theorem beyond the proof. **Pointwise:** complete ladders — every one of the 156 subset-types — at five kingdoms, d = 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, all 775 nonempty values on-formula, with the d = 4 and d = 6 ladders (the even branch and the first composite degree) measured **after** the formula was written. **Blind:** the d = 11 spot trial (4/4 sealed to the digit) and the even-degree corollary's first prediction (§5), a value no odd-degree measurement could have suggested, declared from the proof and then measured. **Symbolic and independent:** both telescope branches verified as polynomial identities, plus a from-scratch independent rebuild of the formula (Auditor environment) reproducing DS₄(d)+1 numerically at all thirteen degrees 3 ≤ d ≤ 15 — including degrees whose lattices have never been computed by anyone.

## 2. Notation and substrate

H⁴_prim(X_d) ⊗ C decomposes as ⊕_a C·v_a over characters a = (a₀,…,a₅), a_i ∈ Z/d∖{0}, Σ a_i ≡ 0 (mod d); the coordinate torus μ_d⁶/diag acts on v_a by a [2]. The fifteen families of standard planes correspond to the perfect matchings of the six coordinates; the d³ planes of a family form a single orbit of its edge-torus. Two facts of the classical Fermat machinery are used as substrate: the eigenbasis description above and its torus equivariance (textbook), and a **Nonvanishing Lemma** — *the class of a standard plane has nonzero coefficient on every character of its support*. This is the explicit period computation of Movasati–Villaflor [5]: the period of a linear algebraic cycle against the eigenform ω_β is a nonzero product of Beta/Jacobi factors precisely on the support (the clean membership form is Villaflor [6, Prop. 2.2]; the eigenbasis itself is Shioda [2], Aoki–Shioda [3]). The Lemma is therefore a cited result, not an assumption; §6 records this.

## 3. The proof — the support calculus

**(a) Master formula.** Averaging a plane class over a subtorus kills every character nontrivial on it; with the Nonvanishing Lemma, the span of one family F (matching M) is ⟨h⟩ ⊕ span{ v_a : a_x ≡ −a_y for every edge (x,y) ∈ M } — the **character support** of F, plus the hyperplane class. Spans of alliances are sums of spans, so

> rank V(S) = 1 + | ∪_{F∈S} supp(F) |.

**(b) Möbius duality.** The Möbius transform of |∪| over the subset lattice is (−1)^{|S|+1}|∩|, and of the constant 1 it is (−1)^{|S|+1}. Hence

> ν(S) = (−1)^{|S|+1} · ( 1 + | ∩_{F∈S} supp(F) | ).

**(c) Counting the common support.** A character a lies in every support of S iff a_x ≡ −a_y along every edge of the union graph G_S — iff a is an alternating ±r labeling on each connected component. A bipartite component admits d−1 such labelings (r ∈ Z/d∖{0}); a non-bipartite component contains an odd closed walk, forcing 2r ≡ 0, whose only nonzero solution r = d/2 exists iff d is even. Multiplying over components gives |∩ supp| = N_d(G_S). The condition Σ a_i ≡ 0 is automatic (matched values cancel in pairs), and every such character is Hodge: each value pair (x, −x) contributes fractional-part sum 1 at every unit t, and three pairs give exactly 3. ∎

**(d) The corollaries this absorbs.** K = 1: a matching is three disjoint edges, three bipartite components, N = (d−1)³ — the flat law rank (d−1)³+1, which this campaign verified at ten degrees (eleven family-tower verifications — d = 10 at both primes). K = 2: a skew pair's union is a single 6-cycle (bipartite), N = d−1, so ν = −d; a pencil pair is a doubled edge plus a 4-cycle, N = (d−1)², so ν = −((d−1)²+1) — both verified at four kingdoms before the proof existed. K ≥ 3, odd d: N ∈ {0, d−1} — the **quantization**, ±1 or ±d and nothing between; N = d−1 exactly when G_S is connected bipartite, which happens precisely when S lies inside the matching-set of one coordinate bipartition. Those matching-sets are the **bipartite hexads**: ten of them (one per 3+3 split), each the six perfect matchings of a K₃,₃ ⊂ K₆, each isomorphic to S₃, no subset of size ≥ 3 lying in two — so the resonant masses at K = 3, 4, 5, 6 are the finger counts 10·C(6,K) = 200, 150, 60, 10, and nothing of size ≥ 7 resonates.

**(e) The choruses and the universal note (the mechanism, exhibited).** For a bipartition β, the d−1 alternating characters span, with ⟨h⟩, a d-dimensional space D_β — the **chorus** — contained in the span of each of the hexad's six families and (for odd d) of no other: a family outside has an edge inside one part, forcing 2r ≡ 0. Interior alliances re-meet the same D_β, and inclusion–exclusion rebounds by +d: resonance is six families repeatedly meeting in one shared chorus. Two different choruses share only ⟨h⟩ — the **universal note**, present in all fifteen spans; its explicit representative is the famsum identity (the sum of a family's d³ plane classes is one and the same vector regardless of the family — verified independently at two kingdoms, all fifteen families). Oriented charge sums of any single hexad family generate its chorus (closed form verified at two kingdoms); the song belongs to the stage.

## 4. The telescope — the formula as pure counting

Summing ν over all nonempty subsets (Möbius inversion):

> rank V(4,d) = 15[(d−1)³+1] − 45[(d−1)²+1] − 60d + 91 + 100(d−1)   (d odd)

with 15 families, 45 pencil and 60 skew pairs, 91 = Σ_{K≥3} (−1)^{K+1} C(15,K) the alternating generic sea, and 100 = 200 − 150 + 60 − 10 the alternating resonant mass. Expansion gives 15d³ − 90d² + 175d − 99 = DS₄(d) + 1 identically. For even d the impostor's contributions (§5) add exactly 15d − 39. Both branches are symbolic identities, and the numerical telescope reproduces every measured cell of this campaign — 21, 142, 401, 1002, 1861, 3302, 5121, 7762, 10901 at d = 3..11 — and predicted rank V(4,13) = 19921 before that cell's first computation.

## 5. The impostor and his audited debt (even degree)

For d even the character (d/2)·(1,1,1,1,1,1) is Hodge and alternates along **every** edge of K₆: one global impostor singing in every choir of every bipartition and in every family span. First consequence, declared from the proof and then measured blind at d = 4: the generic triple whispers ν = +2 (not +1) — together with ν(skew) = −4, ν(pencil) = −10, ν(K₃,₃-triple) = +4, four predictions, four hits, independently reproduced. Second consequence, the even-degree surcharge of DS audited céntimo a céntimo: summing (−1)^{|S|+1}(d−1)^{b(S)} over the alliances with at least one non-bipartite component, the only d-scaling line is the fifteen pencil triples (a bipartite doubled edge paying d−1 beside a K₄ casino where only the impostor sings), contributing 15(d−1); the pure casinos' alternating ledger sums to −24; total **15d − 39** — exactly DS's δ-term, identically.
